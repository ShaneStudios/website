Guidelines

Suggestion Guidelines

I'm always looking for new ways to improve Shane Studios and my projects. Your ideas and suggestions are a vital part of that process. To help me understand your vision, please follow these guidelines when submitting a suggestion.

The Anatomy of a Great Suggestion

A helpful suggestion clearly explains the "what," the "why," and the "how." It moves beyond a simple idea to a well-reasoned proposal.

  • Identify the Problem or Opportunity: Start by explaining the problem you're trying to solve or the opportunity you see. For example, instead of "add a search bar," try "It's difficult to find specific snippets; a search bar on the Snippets page would help."
  • Describe the Proposed Solution: Clearly describe your suggested feature or improvement. What would it do? How would it work from a user's perspective?
  • Explain the Benefit: Why would this be a good change? Explain how your suggestion would improve the user experience, add value, or make the site more useful for you and others.
  • Be Specific: The more detail you can provide, the better. If you have an idea for how a feature should look or behave, please describe it. Mockups or sketches are welcome but not required.

What Kind of Suggestions Are Welcome?

I'm open to a wide range of ideas. Feel free to suggest things like:

  • New Website Features: Ideas for new pages, sections, or interactive elements (e.g., "a dark/light mode toggle," "a comment section for articles").
  • Improvements to Existing Features: Suggestions on how to make a current feature better (e.g., "add syntax highlighting options to the RyxIDE demo," "the article filters should remember my choice").
  • New Content Ideas: Topics for new articles, projects, or code snippets you'd like to see.
  • Usability and Accessibility Improvements: Feedback on how to make the site easier to use for everyone, including those with disabilities.

What Happens After You Submit?

Every suggestion is read and considered carefully. While I can't promise that every idea will be implemented, here is the general process:

  1. Review: I will review your suggestion to understand the core problem and the proposed solution.
  2. Evaluation: I'll evaluate the feasibility, potential impact, and how it aligns with the overall vision for Shane Studios.
  3. Prioritization: Viable suggestions are added to a backlog of potential features and prioritized for future development.

Due to the volume of suggestions, I may not be able to provide a personal response to every email, but please know that your input is greatly appreciated and plays a direct role in shaping the future of this site. Thank you for helping me build a better platform!
